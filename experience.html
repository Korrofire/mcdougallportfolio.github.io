Experiences That Defined Me

My journey towards UX and web design wasn't a straight line. Originally, I planned to get a Computer Science degree and become a game developer. My time within FIRST Robotics and Western Aerospace Scholars (WAS) helped me experience various fields of STEM in high school before I figured out design was the way to go. Nowadays, I like to show off my time in those programs as well as a personal project of mine - streaming! I stream on Twitch when I'm not working or attending classes, showing people the games that continuously inspire me.


FIRST Robotics

In high school, I was a part of the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) for all 4 years. I was on Team 5803 Apex Robotics as one of the founding members and the team's first programmer. The program is all about getting students into system by having them create competitive robots based on a game given out each year. FRC allowed me to fall in love with STEM while learning about code and technical documentation. I expanded into learning about presentations and documentation during my junior year on the team which turned out to be my first experience with how amazing technical communication is. The program is a great way to get exposed to a wonderful community that encourages learning new things and pursuing whatever you want in and out of STEM. The pictures show two robots I helped create: Level Red (left) and Hailstorm (right).


Western Aerospace Scholars

Western Aerospace Scholors is a program with NASA and the Museum of Flight to give high school sophomores and juniors a chance to learn about space and help create a mission to Mars. I did both sophomore and junior levels of the program in 2017 and 2018. The program gives you online college-level papers and projects to prove you can make into the residency portion. It helps you make connections with people within aerospace and STEM as you try create your own Moon base. In the residency portion, I was put into the team tasked at creating manual labor jobs on Mars. By the end, we had to present our work after going on trips to places such as Boeing and University of Washington Seattle. The program was another way I learned about STEM in high school and gave me connections I can still use to this day. Learning about the wonders of space then designing things based on that is one way to learn about the importance of design.
